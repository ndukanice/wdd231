LDS Youth Service Tracker

Site Plan Document

Site Name

LDS Youth Service Tracker

Why this name?

The name "LDS Youth Service Tracker" clearly identifies the target audience (LDS Youth) and the primary function (tracking service projects). It's straightforward, memorable, and immediately communicates the site's purpose to visitors.

Optional Domain: ldyouthservice.org or youthservicetracker.org

Site Purpose

The LDS Youth Service Tracker serves as a comprehensive digital platform designed to empower LDS youth groups in organizing, documenting, and celebrating their service initiatives. The website will:

  • Inspire: Showcase the impact of youth-led service projects through photos, testimonials, and statistics
  • Plan: Provide tools to propose new service projects and view upcoming events via an interactive calendar
  • Track: Enable youth to log completed service hours and visualize their collective community impact
  • Connect: Foster a sense of community by sharing stories and celebrating achievements across wards and stakes

This platform encourages youth to live gospel principles of service while developing leadership and organizational skills through meaningful community engagement.

Scenarios

These scenarios represent questions from typical visitors and guide the website's content development:

Scenario 1: Finding Service Ideas

Question: "What are some service project ideas our youth group could do this month?"

Answer: The Project Planner page will feature a JSON-powered list of service project ideas categorized by season, skill level, and community need (e.g., food drives, park cleanups, care packages for seniors, technology help for elderly, etc.).

Scenario 2: Tracking Group Progress

Question: "How many service hours has our youth group completed this year?"

Answer: The Impact & Gallery page will display a statistics dashboard showing total hours served, number of projects completed, people helped, and other meaningful metrics that visualize the group's collective impact.

Scenario 3: Proposing New Projects

Question: "How can I suggest a new service project for our group?"

Answer: The Project Planner page includes an interactive form where youth can submit project proposals with details about the service opportunity, required resources, and suggested dates.

Color Scheme

The color palette reflects the values of faith, service, and youth energy while maintaining accessibility and readability.

Primary Blue

#2C5F99

Usage: Headers, navigation bar, primary buttons, and main call-to-action elements. Represents faith, trust, and stability.

Accent Gold

#F5A623

Usage: Highlights, hover states, icons, and accent elements. Represents light, hope, and positive energy.

Success Green

#4CAF50

Usage: Success messages, completed projects badges, and positive statistics. Represents growth and achievement.

Background White

#FFFFFF

Usage: Main background, card backgrounds, and content areas. Provides clean, readable space.

Text Dark Gray

#333333

Usage: Body text and paragraph content. Ensures readability with sufficient contrast.

Light Gray

#F8F9FA

Usage: Section backgrounds, borders, and subtle dividers.

Wireframes

The wireframe below shows the planned layout for the home page across both mobile and desktop device sizes.

Mobile View Features (320px - 767px)

  • Hamburger menu navigation
  • Full-width hero image with inspirational quote
  • Stacked content cards
  • Prominent call-to-action buttons
  • Simplified footer with essential links

Desktop View Features (768px+)

  • Horizontal navigation bar
  • Wide hero banner with overlay text
  • Multi-column grid layout (2-3 columns)
  • Sidebar for quick stats
  • Expanded footer with social links and contact info

Home Page Layout Elements

  1. Header: Site logo/title and navigation menu
  2. Hero Section: Inspiring image with scripture or quote about service
  3. Recent Projects: Featured cards showing 2-3 recent youth service initiatives
  4. Quick Stats: Overview of total hours served, projects completed, youth participating
  5. Call to Action: "Propose a Project" and "View Calendar" buttons
  6. Footer: Contact information, social media links, copyright

Target Audience

Primary Users: LDS Youth ages 12-18 and their leaders

Secondary Users: Parents, ward members, and stake leadership

User Needs:

  • Easy-to-use interface accessible on mobile devices
  • Clear visual communication with engaging graphics
  • Quick access to project ideas and calendar
  • Ability to track and celebrate achievements
  • Faith-centered content that inspires service
